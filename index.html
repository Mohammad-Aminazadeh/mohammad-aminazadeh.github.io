About Me

I completed my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from the University of Isfahan (UI), Iran, in 2021. Initially, I pursued Chemical Engineering for the first two years of my undergraduate studies. However, I subsequently shifted my focus to Computer Science and graduated with distinction, ranking among the top students in that department with a GPA of 3.84 (17.80 out of 20) for the final three years studying my new major. In terms of my language proficiency, I recently attained a band score of 7.5 on the IELTS exam.

During my academic journey, I had the privilege of defending my Final Thesis under the guidance of Dr. Noorbehbahani, specializing in the fields of Big Data Mining, Data Analytics, and Machine Learning. My B.Sc. thesis involved time series analysis and clustering of city traffic patterns, to explore locations with similar traffic behaviors to improve urban planning and transportation management. I also examined the impact of weather on traffic, contributing to effective transportation management.

Apart from my academic pursuits, I have also engaged in personal projects that have further enhanced my skills. Notably, I undertook a web scraping project utilizing Selenium, which involved extracting financial data from 26,600 public companies across ten different countries over a ten-year period. Feature engineering and various statistical analysis were done on data.

My passion for research has been evident throughout my academic career. In collaboration with my supervisor, I have co-authored two research papers that have been published in prestigious venues such as the journal "Education and Information Technologies" and the IEEE International Conference on Computer and Knowledge Engineering (ICCKE). Recently my third paper was submitted to IEEE International Conference on Internet of Things and Its Applications (IoT). These publications have received a total of 66 citations.

Projects

  • City Traffic Data Analytics (Time Series Analysis and Visualization - Time Series Clustering)
    • I worked on this real-world project as my final thesis. In Isfahan, traffic sensors collected data for 37 months from 71 intersections, yielding 13 million lines of text-based traffic records. The data was cleaned, aggregated, and properly stored in order to be prepared for further time series and clustering analysis. In order to identify similar or dissimilar locations in the city for more effective traffic management, the main process of this project was to divide city intersections based on the similarity of their traffic flows. My secondary goal was to use time series visualization to examine daily variations in traffic flow at individual intersections and identify patterns. Results obtained from this project led to the publication of my second research paper.

      • Main Tools and Libraries Used: TSLearn - Pandas - Numpy - Matplotlib - Seaborn - Tableau Software - Excel
