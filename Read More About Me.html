About Me

Full Autobiography

Before University

I was born in 1998 in Shiraz, Iran. I earned a math and physics diploma with a grade point average of 18.26 out of 20. After taking "The university entrance exam" (also known as "Konkur") and ranking 1,800 out of 165,000 Math and Physics examiners (Top 10%), I chose to pursue my bachelor's at the University of Isfahan.

My Bachelor's Journey

I studied chemical engineering for the first two years of my bachelor's degree, but I showed little interest in it and received below-average grades. Even as a young child, I had a strong passion for "Problem Solving" and developed an interest in coding. These were the routes that led me to my major change in computer science, which was regarded as a challenging major requiring a wide range of "Problem Solving" and "Critical Thinking" skills. My grades significantly increased as soon as I began studying my new major because I showed such a strong interest in it.

Other Interests and Hobbies

In addition to my academic interests, I consider myself a professional equity investor and am very interested in financial markets in order to find solutions to problems that others may find challenging or impossible to solve. I really enjoy using machine learning to explore knowledge in other academic disciplines.
I used to play water polo and swim professionally. I just work out now to stay healthy.
